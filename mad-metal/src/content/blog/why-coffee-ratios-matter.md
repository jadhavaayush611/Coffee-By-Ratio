If you've ever wondered why your coffee tastes different every morning even though you use the same "two scoops," the answer is simple: you aren't using a ratio.

## Consistency is King

The biggest enemy of a great cup of coffee is inconsistency. Different coffees have different densities. One scoop of a dark roast might weigh 7 grams, while a light roast might weigh 10 grams.

By using a scale and a ratio, you ensure that the *mass* of coffee and water remains the same, regardless of volume.

## The Science of Extraction

Coffee brewing is chemistry. You are trying to extract the right amount of soluble solids from the bean.
- **Under-extraction**: Tastes sour, salty, or thin.
- **Over-extraction**: Tastes bitter, dry, or astringent.
- **Ideal extraction**: Tastes sweet, balanced, and complex.

The ratio controls the "solvent" (water) available to "dissolve" the coffee.
